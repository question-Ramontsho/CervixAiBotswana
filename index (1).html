Study context

Cervical cancer is a leading cause of cancer mortality among women in Botswana, with most cases diagnosed at advanced stages. This prototype demonstrates a Claude Vision AI system for VIA image classification in rural PHC clinics, designed to support clinician decision-making without replacing specialist care.

Research questions

RQ1 — Can the AI accurately classify VIA images into normal, precancerous, or suspected cancer categories?
RQ2 — Does CNN-assisted tele-screening reduce average referral completion time?
RQ3 — How do patients and PHC staff perceive usability of AI-assisted screening?
RQ4 — Is CNN-assisted tele-screening feasible in low-resource rural PHC clinics?

Study hypotheses

H1 — The AI model achieves sensitivity ≥85% and specificity ≥85% vs expert assessment
H2 — AI tele-screening reduces referral time by ≥30 days vs standard care
H3 — Patients and staff report >80% positive feedback on usability
